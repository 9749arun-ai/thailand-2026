    OFF-TRIP IDEAS nearby islands
    Koh Racha Yai
    from Phuket · 40-min speedboat
    Ao Tawan Tok beach is powder-white, often empty on weekdays. Snorkel the south headland. Day-trip (1,500 THB boat) or overnight at The Racha resort for the empty-beach sunrise.
    Koh Yao Yai
    from Phuket / Krabi · 50-min ferry
    Bigger sister of Koh Yao Nai, even quieter. Loh Paret Beach and Loh Jaak Bay. Rent a scooter. 1\u20132 nights, combine with Koh Yao Nai on a Yao-loop.
    Similan Islands
    from Phuket (Khao Lak pier) · 1.5h speedboat
    Closes May 15 every year. Window: only until mid-May 2026. National park, turquoise, Donald Duck Bay. Day-trip ~3,500 THB, includes lunch. Best snorkel day of your life.
    Koh Phi Phi
    from Phuket · 2h ferry
    Maya Bay + Monkey Beach. Overnight to avoid day-tripper crowds \u2014 stay on Phi Phi Don. 1 night is enough unless partying. Good viewpoint hike at sunrise.
    Koh Nang Yuan
    from Koh Tao · 15-min longtail
    Three-islands-joined-by-sand. The drone shot you've seen on every Thailand postcard. Go at 7am. 200 THB entry, day trip from Mae Haad.
    Ang Thong Marine Park
    from Ko Phangan · 1h speedboat
    42-island archipelago. Emerald Lagoon, panoramic viewpoint hike. The inspiration for The Beach novel. Day-trip 2,500 THB, kayaks included.
    Khao Sok National Park
    from Phuket/Surat · 3h drive
    Older than the Amazon. Cheow Lan Lake floating bungalows. Early-morning jungle boat, monkeys, hornbills. 1\u20132 night detour between Phuket and Phangan.

    April–May: hot & dry (32–36°C). Andaman Sea: calm & crystal clear. Peak season ends late May.
